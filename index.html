Hello world!

The time has arrived for the universe to know about me. I have been a post-doctoral researcher until Nobember 2024 in the Verification group of Université Libre de Bruxelles(ULB) under Jean-François Raskin. Before joining ULB, I was a CNRS-funded PhD student, at LMF in Ens Paris-Saclay, France, under the supervision of Patricia Bouyer-Decitre and Nathalie Bertrand (Inria Rennes).

Earlier, I did my M.Sc. in Computer Science, and B.Sc. in Mathematics and Computer Science from the cute Chennai Mathematical Institute, India. I did my M1 internship with Denis Kuperberg in the Plume team at Ens Lyon. I did my M2 thesis under M. Praveen at CMI.

Change is the only constant, and learning is the key behind that. My current research also focuses on "Learning". While "Machine Learning" is the current trend in modern computer science, my research mainly focuses on the learning of "Automata", a type of machine, using the tools from "Formal Methods".

Yes, Formal Methods is my main area of interest. If you ask me to be more specific, I would say games on (finite) graphs, verification of parameterized systems, probabilistic systems, etc. In my PhD, we introduced and studied a model of games that aims to deal with a parameterized number of players. Sounds exciting? Feel free to read my PhD thesis, and contact me for any questions or comments.

To dig more into my past and present, please look into my CV.

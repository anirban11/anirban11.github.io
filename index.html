Hello world!

The time has arrived for the universe to learn about me. I am currently a post-doctoral researcher in the verification group of Université Libre de Bruxelles(ULB) under Jean-François Raskin. Before joining ULB, I was a PhD student at LMF in Ens Paris-Saclay, France, under the supervision of Patricia Bouyer-Decitre and Nathalie Bertrand (Inria Rennes).

Earlier, I did my M.Sc. in Computer Science, and B.Sc. in Mathematics and Computer Science from Chennai Mathematical Institute, India. I did my M1 internship with Denis Kuperberg in the Plume team at Ens Lyon.

Change is the only constant, and learning is the key behind that. My current research also focuses on "Learning". While "Machine Learning" is the two magic words that every computer scientist wants to be their children's first words, my research mainly focuses on the learning of "Automata", a type of machine, using the tools from "Formal Methods".

The other areas of my interests are games on (finite) graphs, verification of parameterized systems, probabilistic systems, etc. In my PhD, we introduced and studied a model of games that aims to deal with a parameterized number of players. Sounds exciting? Feel free to read my PhD thesis, and contact me for any questions or comments.

To dig more into my past and present, please look into my CV.
